## How Do Kratom and Kava Differ From Each Other?

Kratom and kava come from different plants, different regions, and work through different mechanisms. Grouping them together can be misleading.

**Kratom (Mitragyna speciosa):**
- Grows in Southeast Asia (Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia)
- Active compounds: mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine
- Interacts with opioid receptors in the brain
- Effects range from stimulating (low doses, 1-3 grams) to sedating (higher doses, 5-8 grams)
- Duration: typically 4-6 hours
- Carries dependency risk with regular use

**Kava (Piper methysticum):**
- Grows in the South Pacific (Fiji, Vanuatu, Tonga)
- Active compounds: kavalactones
- Works on GABA receptors (similar pathway to anti-anxiety medications)
- Effects are primarily calming and muscle-relaxing
- Duration: typically 2-4 hours
- Lower dependency risk, but liver concerns exist with poor-quality products

Here's the difference: kratom acts more like a stimulant or mild opioid depending on dose. Kava acts more like a mild sedative. Combining them creates a push-pull effect that some people enjoy but others find unpredictable.

## Is the Feel Free Kratom and Kava Drink Safe?

Safety depends on several factors: how often you use it, what other substances you combine it with, and your personal health profile.

**What matters here:**

Kratom carries a real risk of dependency with daily use. This isn't speculation — it's well-documented in clinical literature and widely reported by regular users. If you drink Feel Free every day for weeks, your body can develop tolerance and withdrawal symptoms when you stop. Withdrawal feels similar to mild opioid withdrawal: irritability, muscle aches, insomnia, and anxiety.

Kava, when sourced properly from noble kava varieties, has a long history of traditional use in Pacific Island cultures. The liver toxicity concerns from the early 2000s were largely traced to products using non-noble kava cultivars or parts of the plant (stems and leaves) that shouldn't be consumed. Quality matters enormously with kava.

**Situations where extra caution is warranted:**
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding (avoid both kratom and kava)
- Liver conditions (kava may worsen liver function)
- Taking prescription medications, especially opioids, benzodiazepines, or antidepressants
- History of substance dependency
- Under 18 years old

Put simply: occasional use by healthy adults appears to carry lower risk. Daily use is where problems tend to start, particularly because of kratom's dependency potential.

## Can You Build a Tolerance to the Feel Free Kratom and Kava Drink?

Yes. Tolerance develops primarily from the kratom component, and it can happen faster than most people expect.

Regular kratom use — even at moderate doses — can lead to tolerance within 1-2 weeks of daily consumption. This means you need more to feel the same effects. That escalation is exactly how dependency starts.

**Signs of developing tolerance:**
- The usual amount stops working as well
- You start drinking more frequently or in larger amounts
- You feel "off" on days you skip
- Irritability or restlessness between doses

**A safer approach to avoid tolerance:**
- Limit use to 2-3 times per week maximum
- Take regular breaks (at least 3-4 consecutive days off per week)
- Never increase your dose to chase the original effect
- Rotate with other relaxation methods (exercise, meditation, kava-only products)

The thing to know: kava tolerance develops much more slowly than kratom tolerance. Some regular kava users actually report "reverse tolerance," where they need less over time to feel effects. This is one reason why kava-only products may be a better fit for frequent use.
